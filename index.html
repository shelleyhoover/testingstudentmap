Chicago in Isolation

Where I went to understand Chicago during a pandemic.

I moved to Chicago on August 16, 2020, in the middle of a pandemic with no connection to the city except a graduate program I knew I had to get through.

For several months, my only experiences were solitary ones. I would visit the city’s landmarks, the lakefront, the campus, all alone as a way to orient myself in someplace new. I forced myself to order pickup from a restaurant several bus stop or train rides away to force myself to understand the “L” and memorize my bus routes. While the only human connections I had were those temporary boxes on Zoom, the city, itself, became my first friend, encouraging me to trudge through snow, jaywalk at the right times, sit and watch the world go by.

These are some of the places that oriented me to a new home, before my new friends could.
